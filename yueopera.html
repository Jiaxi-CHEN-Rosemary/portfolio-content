Yue Opera

Yue Opera is the second most popular opera form in China. Originating in Shaoxing, Zhejiang in about 1900, Yue Opera emphasizes singing, performing, and staging. As Yue opera uses is performed speaking in a variant of Wu, it is most popular in Wu-speaking areas including southern Jiangsu, Zhejiang and Shanghai. My mom like this opera term the most and has taken me to the theatre several times to enjoy the live when I was kid.



Though sharing many common characteristics, especially in terms of singing, with Hu Opera, Yue Opera features classic stories such as The Dream of the Red Chamber, The Peony Pavilion, and The Butterfly Lovers.
